Treaty 3

Treaties are agreements made between the government of Canada and indigenous groups

Signed between queen Elizabeth and the Saulteaux tribe of the Ojibbeway at the northwest angle on the lake of the woods in Ontario and eastern Manitoba.

Treaty 3 was released October 3,1873



Map

Treaty 3 includes large parts of what is now northwestern Ontario and a small part of eastern Manitoba. Communities that share the benefits and obligations of treaty No. 3 include, Falcon Lake, Middlebro, Point du Bois, etc . Most of Whiteshell Provincial Heritage park is within the treaty 3 area as well. Buffalo point first nation was also entered into treaty 3.
