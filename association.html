Association

Communities that share in the benefits and obligations of Treaty No. 3 include, Falcon Lake, Middlebro, Point du Bois, etc. Much of Whiteshell Provincial Heritage Park is within the Treaty 3 area as well. Buffalo point first nation was also entered into treaty 3 (Southern Chiefs' Organization Inc).

Treaty #3 existed in the territory of the Anishinaabe Nation, key to Canadian Confederation in that the British wanted to plan a route between Fort Garry and Fort William (now Winnipeg, MB and Thunder Bay, ON respectively) (our-nation).

Canadian Confederation

The Anishinaabe held that they would not cede lands, or allow farming or settlement in their territory. Treaty 3 is 55,000sq. spanning from west of thunder bay to north of Sioux lookout, along the international border to province of Manitoba- link It is made up of 28 first nation communities, with a population of approximately 25,000.

Under the terms of the treaty, the Government of Canada promised to set aside reserves for the Saulteaux (Ojibwe) and to provide them with various monetary awards, including a one-time cash payment of $12 per family of five and a yearly payment of $5 per person.
